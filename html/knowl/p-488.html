For the same students who like linear algebra, this gives us a specific understanding of the Laplace transform. Let \(EOF(0,\infty)\) be the space of piecewise-continuous functions of exponential order defined on \((0, \infty)\) (\(EOF\) for exponential order functions). Let \(DF^+\) be the set of continuous functions which are defined on \((a, \infty)\) for some \(a > 0\) and have limit \(0\) as \(s \rightarrow \infty\) (DF for decay functions). These are both linear spaces, since addition, subtraction and multiplication by constants preserve the properties of definition. The the Laplace transform is a linear map

\begin{equation*} \calL : EOF(0,\infty) \rightarrow DF^+\text{.} \end{equation*}
