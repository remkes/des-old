The linear DE is called homogeneous if \(Q(t) = 0\text{.}\) In the homogeneous case, the DE is relatively easy to solve as a seperable equation.

\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dt} \amp = -P(t) y\\ \frac{1}{y} dy \amp = -P(t) dt\\ \int \frac{1}{y} dy \amp = -\int P(t) dt\\ \ln |y| \amp = -\int P(t) dt + c\\ y \amp = c e^{-\int P(t)} dt \end{align*}
