If the positive integer values of the \(\Gamma\) function generalize the factorial, what can be expect for other values? The results are surprising. Look at the value of \(\Gamma(\frac{1}{2})\) (in this calculation, we use a well-known result for the integral \(e^{-u^2}\)).

\begin{align*} \Gamma \left( \frac{1}{2} \right) \amp = \int_0^\infty x^{-\frac{1}{2}} e^{-x} dx\\ x \amp = u^2\\ dx \amp = 2u du\\ x^{-\frac{1}{2}} \amp = \frac{1}{u}\\ \amp = 2 \int_0^\infty e^{-u^2} u \frac{1}{u} du\\ \amp = 2 \int_0^\infty e^{-u^2}\\ \amp = 2 \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2} = \sqrt{\pi} \end{align*}
