Finally, it lets us understand inverse Lapalce transforms of products.

\begin{align*} \calL^{-t} \left\{ \frac{k^2}{(s^2+k^2)^2} \right\} \amp = \calL^{-1} \left\{ \frac{k}{s^2+k^2} \frac{k}{s^2+k^2} \right\}\\ \amp = \sin kt \star \sin kt\\ \amp = \int_0^t \sin ku \sin (kt-ku) du\\ \amp \text{ Using } \left[ \sin A \sin B = \frac{1}{2} \cos (A-B) - \cos (A+B) \right]\\ \calL^{-t} \left\{ \frac{k^2}{(s^2+k^2)^2} \right\} \amp = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^t \cos (ku - kt + ku) - \cos (ku + kt - ku ) du\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^t \cos (2ku - 2t) - \cos (kt) du\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2} \left. \frac{\sin (2ku - kt)}{2k} \right|_0^t - \left. \frac{1}{2} u \cos (kt) \right|_0^t\\ \amp = \frac{\sin (2kt-kt) - \sin (-kt)}{4k} - \frac{t\cos kt}{2}\\ \amp = \frac{\sin (kt)}{2k} - \frac{t\cos kt}{2} = \frac{\sin kt - kt \cos kt}{2k} \end{align*}
