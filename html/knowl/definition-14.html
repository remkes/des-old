Definition 2.1.3.

An equation is called linear if it has the form

\begin{equation*} a_n(t)y^{(n)} + a_{n-1}(t)y^{(n-1)} + \ldots + a_1(t) y^\prime + a_0(t) y = f(t)\text{.} \end{equation*}

In addition, a linear equation is called homogenous if the \(f(t)\) term is simply \(f(t) = 0\text{.}\)
