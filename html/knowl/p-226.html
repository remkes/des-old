As an aside: the sums of \(\sin\) and \(\cos\) can be expressed as a single sinusoidal wave. We are helped by a relatively obscure but useful identity (if \(a \geq 0)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} a \sin t + b \cos t = \sqrt{a^2+b^2} \sin (t + \phi) \hspace{1cm} \text{ where } \hspace{1cm} \phi = \arcsin \left( \frac{b}{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}} \right) \end{equation*}
