After these initial examples, two of questions seem natural. First, we might observe that \(P\) and \(Q\) were, so far, only rational functions, which means that we only need to deal with polynomials in the calculation. What if \(P\) and \(Q\) were other analytic functions? In this cases, we would have to expand \(P\) and \(Q\) as series about the ordinary point and then multiply them by the series for \(y\) in the calculation. This is possible, but miserable.
