Recall the term \(b^2 -4ac\) is called the discriminant. In terms of the discriminat, there are three cases. We are making use here of the fundamental theorem of arithmetic, which says that polynoials always have roots over \(\CC\text{.}\) See [cross-reference to target(s) "complex-numbers" missing or not unique] for reference.

  1. \(b^2 -4ac > 0 \implies\) two real roots.

  2. \(b^2 -4ac = 0 \implies\) one real repeated root.

  3. \(b^2 -4ac \lt 0 \implies\) two complex roots.
