Now I consider the initial conditions \(u(x,0) = f(x)\text{.}\) For each \(n\text{,}\) the seperable solution is a sine wave \(\sin (nx)\text{.}\) This is great if \(f(x)\) happened to be such a sine wave, but I would like the theory to work with more general initial conditions. The key observation here is that the heat equation is linear: if there two solutions to the heat equation, their sum is also a solution. Therefore, I could add up these seperable solutions. For example, a possible solution could be this function.

\begin{equation*} u(x,t) = 4e^{-9kt} \sin 3t + 6e^{-25kt} \sin 5t \end{equation*}
