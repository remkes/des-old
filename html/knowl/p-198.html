The first substitution is for a class of terribly named DEs called homogenous equations. Please note, this homogeneous has nothing to do with the previous definition for linear equations. The word here comes from a different use of the term in algebra. In any case, a homogenous DE has the form

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dt} = f \left( \frac{y}{t} \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
