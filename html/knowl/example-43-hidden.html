In this example, the resulting integrals are not expressable by elementary functions. In these cases, we just leave the integrals in the final solution. We just have to live with these unexpressible function (or, I suppose, come up with new names for them.)

\begin{align*} y^{\prime\prime} - y \amp = \frac{1}{t}\\ r^2 - 1 \amp = 0\\ r \amp = \pm 1\\ y_1 \amp = e^t\\ y_2 \amp = e^{-t}\\ y_1^\prime \amp = e^t\\ y_2^\prime \amp = - e^{-t}\\ W \amp = -1 -1 = -2\\ u_1 \amp = \int \frac{-y_2 f}{W} dt = -\int \frac{ e^{-t} \frac{1}{t}}{-2} = \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{e^{-t}}{t} dt\\ u_2 \amp = \int \frac{y_1 f}{W} dt = \int \frac{ e^t \frac{1}{t}}{-2} = \frac{-1}{2} \int \frac{e^t}{t} dt\\ y_p \amp = \frac{e^t}{2} \int \frac{e^{-t}}{t} dt - \frac{e^{-t}}{2} \int \frac{e^t}{t} dt\\ y \amp = \frac{e^t}{2} \int \frac{e^{-t}}{t} dt - \frac{e^{-t}}{2} \int \frac{e^t}{t} dt + Ae^t + Be^{-t} \end{align*}
