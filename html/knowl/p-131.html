As an interpretation, Lipschitz continuity is a control on the global growth over the a specific interval. For a Lipschitz continuous function, there is a linear function that bounds the function on the designated interval. Unsurprisingly, this means that the defintion usually only works on bounded sets. For a more visual interpretation, the definition compares \(f\) to some other linear function \(g(x) = K|x|\text{.}\) The graph of \(g\) gives a cone in \(\RR^2\) and the \(f\) must stay inside this cone over an interval to be Lipschitz continuous. In this way, the definition limits the local growth of \(f\) and its derivatives.
