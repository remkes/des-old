A second order linear differential operator is an operator of the form.

\begin{equation*} L = a(t) \frac{d^2}{dt^2} + b(t) \frac{d}{dt} + c(t)\text{.} \end{equation*}

In operator notation, a second order linear differential equation is \(Ly = f(t)\text{.}\) The principles of linearity and superposition defined for first order equation in SectionÂ 2.6 still hold for higher-order linear equations.
