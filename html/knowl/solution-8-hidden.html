First, the functions are undefined when \(y-0\text{,}\) so there is a family above the \(x\) axis and a family below the \(x\) axis. I can see from the slope field that these mirror each other. In the first and fourth quadrants, the functions diverge away from the \(x\) axis and look to approach horizontal asymptotes as \(x \rightarrow \infty\) (though this may simply be very slow growth). Their domains look like \((a, \infty)\text{,}\) where \(a\) is a starting point on the \(x\) axis, and their ranges seem to be \((0, \pm h)\text{,}\) where \(h\) is the \(y\) value of the asymptotes. For the second and third quadrants, the functions start from horizontal asymptotes and diverge toward the \(x\) axis. Their domains seem to be \((-\infty, -a)\text{,}\) where \(-a\) is a point on the \(x\) axis, and their ranges seem to be \((0, \pm h)\) where \(h\) is the height of the asymptote. If I connect the positive and negative pieces, I get horizontal quartic-like paths.
