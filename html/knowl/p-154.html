Consider the \(f(x) = x^2\text{.}\) The derivaitve is \(2x\text{,}\) which is bounded locally near \(0\text{.}\) \(f(x) = x^\frac{2}{3}\) has derivative \(\frac{2}{3} x^{\frac{-1}{3}}\) which is unbounded near 0. The former function is Lipschitz continuous and the later is not.
