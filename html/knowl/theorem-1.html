Theorem 1.5.4. Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.

Let \(p(x)\) be a polynomial of degree \(d\) with real or complex coefficients. Then \(p(x)\) has exactly \(d\) roots in the complex numbers (though some may be repeated). Equivalently, \(p(x)\) factors completely over the complex numbers: there are complex numbers \(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_d\text{,}\) not necessariliy distinct from each other, such that

\begin{equation*} p(x) = (x-\alpha_i)(x-\alpha_2)\ldots(x-\alpha_d)\text{.} \end{equation*}
