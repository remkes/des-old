We have two constants, but we want a continuous solution. (It will fail to be differentiable at \(t=1\text{,}\) but that's alright. There is a sudden change in the situation, so that's expected.) Continuity at \(1\) means that \(e-c_1 = c_2\text{.}\) The final solution is a continuous piecewise function.

\begin{equation*} y = \left\{ \begin{matrix} 1 - ce^{-t} \amp t \in [0,1] \\ (e-c)e^{-t} \amp t \in (1,\infty) \end{matrix} \right. \end{equation*}
