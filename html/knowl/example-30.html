Example 2.7.4.
\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dt} \amp = y(ty^3 -1) = ty^4 - y\\ \frac{dy}{dt} + y \amp = ty^4\\ v \amp = y^{-3}\\ \frac{dv}{dt} - 3v \amp = -3t\\ P(t) \amp = -3\\ Q(t) \amp = -3t\\ \mu(t) \amp = e^{\int P(t) dt} = e^{-3t}\\ \frac{d}{dt} e^{-3t} v \amp = -3t e^{-3t}\\ e^{-3t} v \amp = \int -3t e^{-3t} dt = -3 \left( \frac{te^{-3t}}{-3} - \int \frac{e^{-3t}{-3}} dt \right)\\ e^{-3t} v \amp = t e^{-3t} - \int e^{-3t} dt = t e^{-3t} + \frac{e^{-3t}}{3} + c\\ v = \amp t + \frac{1}{3} + ce^{3t} = \frac{3t + 1 + ce^{3t}}{3}\\ y^{-3} \amp = \frac{3t + 1 + ce^{3t}}{3}\\ y^3 \amp = \frac{3}{3t + 1 + ce^{3t}}\\ y \amp = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3}{3t + 1 + ce^{3t}}} \end{align*}
