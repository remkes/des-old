Item. Case 2:.

If \(r_1 - r_2 \in \NN\) but \(r_1 \neq r_2\text{,}\) then the second root may not produce a new linearly independent solution. If it doesn't, and both roots produce the solution \(y_1\text{,}\) then a second solution exists and has the form:

\begin{equation*} y_2 = a y_1 \ln t + \sum_{n=0}^\infty b_n t^{n+r_2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

In this case, I would need to do extra work to find the constant \(a\) and the series coefficients \(b_n\text{.}\)
