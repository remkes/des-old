Item 3.

Let \(a\) be a real constant and consider this differential equation. (6).

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dt} = \frac{1}{y-a} \end{equation*}

  1. What do the existence and uniqueness theorems say about solutions to this DE with initial value \(y(0) = a\text{?}\)

  2. Solve the DE (with the initial condition) and describe what happens to the solution as it approaches \(y=a\text{.}\) Is the solution defined at a point where \(y=a\text{,}\) even though that would lead to division by zero in the DE?
