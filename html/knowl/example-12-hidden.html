\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = y \end{equation*}

The slope at a point \((x,y)\) is \(y\text{,}\) so the slope at \((3,4)\) is \(4\text{,}\) the slope at \((-2,-3)\) is \(-3\) and the slope at \((32,0)\) is \(0\text{.}\) Figure 2.2.10 shows the direction field.

The solutions exactly fit the direction field. Therefore, if we can draw and understand the direction field, we get a sense of the solutions. Notice that since the direction field fills \(\RR^2\) (or a portion of it), we expect an infinite family of graphs of functions to match all the slopes.
