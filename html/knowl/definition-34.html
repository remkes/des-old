Definition 4.4.1.

A singular point \(t_0\) of this DE is called a regular singular point if the two functions \((t-t_0)P(t)\) and \((t-t_0)^2 Q(t)\) are analytic at \(t-0\text{.}\) Equivalently, \(t_0\) is a regular singular point if the limits of \((t-t_0) P(t)\) and \((t-t_0)^2 Q(t)\) as \(t \rightarrow t_0\) exist and are finite. (In complex variables terminology, \(P(t)\) and \(Q(t)\) have to have poles, not essential singaularities, the pole of \(P\) has to be order 1, and the pole of \(Q\) has to be order 1 or 2.) Note that \(P\) and \(Q\) don't have to be rational functions for this definition.
