We know that \(tP(t)\) and \(t^2 Q(t)\) are analytic, from the definition of a regular singular point. This means that \(P(t)\) and \(Q(t)\) can be written in the form

\begin{equation*} P(t) = \frac{p_{-1}}{t} + \sum_{n=0}^\infty p_n t^n \hspace{2cm} Q(t) = \frac{q_{-2}}{t^2} + \frac{q_{-1}}{t} + \sum_{n=0}^\infty q_n t^n\text{.} \end{equation*}
