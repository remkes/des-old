The Navier-Stokes equation is the fundamental equation of fluid dynamics. There are several forms, but we'll state the form for incompressible flows. In this equation, \(u\) is the field describing the fluid flow as a function on \(x,y,z,t\) (position and time), \(\nabla\) is a differential operator, \(P\) is the pressure of the fluid, \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid, and \(\nu\) is the viscosity of the fluid.

\begin{equation*} \frac{\del u}{\del t} + u \cdot \nabla u = - \frac{\nabla P}{\rho} + \nu \nabla^2 u\text{.} \end{equation*}
