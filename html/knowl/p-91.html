A direction field give a qualitative sense of the solutions of the differential equation, since those solutions are functions whose graphs must match the direction field. I can even draw curves that following the directions of the direction field. Such curves are called integral curves and they give a good picture of the expected solutions. This is best seen in examples. The first example is an autonomous equation, just to show that I can also apply this more complicated technique to autonomous equations.
