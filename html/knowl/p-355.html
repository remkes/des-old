Expressing the coefficients in closed form is more difficult than before, but we still can intuite the general form. We use some nice factorial tricks to express the coefficients.

\begin{align*} c_{3n} \amp = \frac{(-1)^n (1)(4)(7)\ldots(3n-2)}{(3n)!} c_0\\ c_{3n+1} \amp = \frac{(-1)^n (2)(5)(8)\ldots(3n-1)}{(3n+1)!} c_1\\ c_{3n+2} \amp = 0 \end{align*}
