Theorem 4.5.5.

Consider a second order homogeneous linear differential equation.

\begin{equation*} y^{\prime \prime} + P(t) y^\prime + Q(t) = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

If \(t_0\) is a regular singular point of this equation, then there exists at least one (but possibly two) \(r \in \RR\) such that the differential equation has a series solution of this form (with \(c_0 \neq 0)\)).

\begin{equation*} y = (t-t_0)^r \sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n (t-t_0)^n \end{equation*}

(Note that \(r\) can be any real number here: this doesn't need to be an integer power).
