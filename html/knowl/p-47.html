More holistically, a differential equation asks the question: what function satisfies a given relationship? In algebra, we are familiar with this question for numbers. \(t + 5 = 7\) implicitly asks which number, when replacing \(t\text{,}\) satisfies the equation. The answer, of course, is \(t=2\text{;}\) the solution to the equation gives the correct replacement. For differential equations, we ask ‘what function’ instead of ‘what number’.
