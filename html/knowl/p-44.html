In differential equation, the name of the independent variable is almost always either \(x\) or \(t\text{.}\) \(x\) is conventional, of course, in calculus; it is also useful for certain geometric interpretation where we want \(x\) and \(y\) to correspond to the familiar cartesian axes. We will use \(x\) as the independnet variable at certain points in the courses, particularly when we use these geometric techniques.
