Let \(f(t)\) be the square wave, which has period 2. Then we can use \(f\) as a forcing term and solve the following DE with a Laplace transform (we factor \(1-e^{-2s}\) as a difference of squares in this calculation).

\begin{align*} y^\prime + by \amp = f(t)\\ sY + bY \amp = \frac{1}{1-e^{-2s}} \int_0^2 f(t) e^{-st} dt\\ \amp = \frac{1}{1-e^{-2s}} \int_0^1 e^{-st} dt\\ \amp = \frac{1}{1-e^{-2s}} \left. \frac{e^{-st}}{-s} \right|_0^1 = \frac{-(e^{-s}-1)}{s(1-e^{-2s})}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{s(1+e^{-s})}\\ Y \amp = \frac{1}{s(s+b)(1-(-e^{-s}))} \end{align*}
