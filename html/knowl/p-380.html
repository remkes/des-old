We might also wonder why we restricted to homogeneous cases. What happens if we add a forcing term \(f(t)\text{?}\) We can certainly do this if \(f\) is analytic on the same domain as \(P\) and \(Q\text{.}\) In all our examples so far, we compared the coefficients to \(0\text{.}\) If we have a forcing terms, we would instead compare the coefficients to the coefficient of \(f\) instead of \(0\text{.}\) This leads to more complications, since the relationship between the coefficients may no longer be a linear recurrence relation.
