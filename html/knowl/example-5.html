Example 2.1.8.
\begin{equation*} \frac{d^2f}{dt^2} + 9 f = 0 \end{equation*}

This is solved by \(\sin 3t\) and also by \(\cos 3t\text{.}\) Moreover, any linear combination \(a\sin 3t + b \cos 3t\) is a solution. In a second order equation, I expect two linearly independent solutions and the general solution is a linear combination of the two linearly independent solutions.
