We translate these thermodynamic realities into a partial differential equation. The second derivative in position measure concavity and the first derivative in time measures the change in heat resulting from the diffusion. The above set-up shows that these should be proportional to each other. Let \(k\) be a positive number.

\begin{equation*} \frac{\del u}{\del t} = k \frac{\del^2 u}{\del x^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
