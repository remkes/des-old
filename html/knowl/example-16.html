Example 2.2.24.
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x}{y} \end{equation*}

This is solved by \(y = \pm \sqrt{c-x^2}\text{,}\) which gives a series of circles. The direction field shows the bounded, relatively stable behaviour which is confirmed by the solutions. Notice that these solution have finite domains: I am not guaranteed solutions with domains that include all real numbers.
