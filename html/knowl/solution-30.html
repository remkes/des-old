Solution 3.5.2.1.

  1. First I write and solve the characteristic equation.

    \begin{equation*} r^2 + 4r + 20 = 0 \implies r = -2 \pm \sqrt{-4} \end{equation*}
    The root are complex with a negative real part. \(\alpha = -2\) and \(\beta = 4\text{.}\) Therefore, the homogeneous solutions are sinusoidal with exponentially decay in amplitude.
    \begin{equation*} y = A e^{-2t} \cos 4t + B e^{-2t} \sin 4t \end{equation*}
    I also need the derivatives of the homogeneous solution. (I've grouped like terms after taking the derivative).
    \begin{equation*} y^\prime = (-2A + 4B) e^{-2t} \cos 4t + (-4A -2B) e^{-2t} \sin 4t \end{equation*}
    Then I use the initial conditions.
    \begin{align*} y(0) \amp = A = 2 \\ y^\prime(0) \amp = (-2A + 4B) = 6 -4 + 4B = 6 \implies B = \frac{10}{4} = \frac{5}{2} \end{align*}
    The solutions to the resulting system doesn't require much work, sinc ethe first initial condition gives \(A\) directly and I used the second to calculate \(B\text{.}\) The specific solution is
    \begin{equation*} y = 2 e^{-2t} \cos 4t + \frac{5}{2} e^{-2t} \sin 4t \end{equation*}

  2. Critically damped oscillations happen when \(\beta = 0\text{,}\) or equivalently when \(b^2 - 4ac=0\text{.}\) In this system, since \(b=4\) and \(a = 1\text{,}\) the coefficient of \(y\) has to be \(c=4\text{.}\)

  3. Exponential decay will happen below the \(c=4\) threshold, when \(b^2 -4ac\) is positive again and the solutions will be real exponentials.

  4. In a harmonic system, you are changing the stiffness of the system. Decreasing the stiffness makes the system less responsive and less likely to have oscilations. For circuits, you are effect the. capacitance, which is (in some ways) analogous to stiffness in the circuit system.
