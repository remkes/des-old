In differential equations, the name of the independent variable is almost always either \(x\) or \(t\text{.}\) \(x\) is conventional, of course, in calculus; it is also useful for certain geometric interpretation where we want \(x\) and \(y\) to correspond to the familiar cartesian axes. I will use \(x\) as the independnet variable at certain points in the courses, particularly when I use these geometric techniques.
