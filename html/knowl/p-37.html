\(\CC\) is identified with the cartesian plane, where \(1\) is \((1,0)\) and \(\imath\) is \((0,1)\text{.}\) The horizontal axis is called the real axis and the vertical axis is called the imaginary axis. The number \(a + bi\) is treated as the coordinate \((a,b)\) in the plane. The inversion of a complex number (non-zero) is

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{a+ b \imath } = \frac{a-b\imath}{a^2+b^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
