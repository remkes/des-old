Finally, I'll define a third important constant.

\begin{equation*} \lambda \defeq \frac{b}{2m} \end{equation*}

If I look back to the underdamped case, the exponential decay term can be written \(e^{-\lambda t}\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\lambda\) is called the decay coefficient. With these new constants, the roots of the characteristic equation become \(r = -\lambda +\pm \imath \sqrt{\omega^2 - \lambda^2}\) and I can rewrite the homogeneous differential equation as

\begin{equation*} y^{\prime \prime} + 2\lambda y^\prime + \omega^2 y = 0 \text{.} \end{equation*}

Now return to the idea of a sinusoidal forcing term \(f(t) = F\sin \gamma t\) with some frequency \(\gamma\text{.}\) I want to look at four situations to understand the effect of this force and the possibility of resonance.
