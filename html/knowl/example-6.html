Example 2.1.9.
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dt} = t \sqrt{y} \end{equation*}

This is solved by \(y = (\frac{t^2}{4} + c)^2\text{,}\) which is a nice family with one real parameter. However, this is also solved by \(y=0\text{,}\) even though it isn't in the family. It's important to be aware that this situation can occur. There is even a name for such a solution.
