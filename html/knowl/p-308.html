Let \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) be solutions to the homogeneous equation \(Ly=0\text{.}\) The general solution to the homogeneous equation is \(Ay_1 + By_2\text{.}\) As with the first-order case, variation of parameters replaces the constants \(A\) and \(B\) with functions. That is, we look for a solution which has the form (where \(u_1(t)\) and \(u_2(t)\) are unknown functions)

\begin{equation*} y_p = u_1 y_1 + u_2 y_2\text{.} \end{equation*}
