Definition 2.1.1.

A differential equation is any equation involving a function \(f(t)\) and its derivatives \(f^\prime(t)\text{,}\) \(f^{\prime \prime}(t)\text{,}\) etc. The highest derivative in the equation is called the order of the equation. A DE which involves partial derivatives is called a partial differential equation; otherwise, it is called an ordinary differtial equation.
