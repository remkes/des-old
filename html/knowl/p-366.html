We assumed that \(k \in \NN\text{.}\) Let's consider two cases: \(k\) even or \(k\) odd. If \(k\) is odd, then the term involving \(c_1\) eventually has \((k-k)\) in the numerator. That means the numerator will be zero: this recurrence relationship eventually stop and we get a polynomial solution. If \(k\) is even, then terms involving \(c_0\) eventually have \((k-k)\) in the numerator, and again we get a polynomial solution. So, for any \(k\text{,}\) we find a polynomial solution. (Note this extends the expected radius of convergence. We were only guaranteed a radius of \(R=1\text{,}\) but polynomials converge everywhere, so we get \(R=\infty\)).
