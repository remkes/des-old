Having used friction to interpret the constant \(b\text{,}\) the only piece that remains to interpret is the function \(f(t)\) in a non-homogeneous SOCCLDe. In the harmonic motion intepretation so far, all these terms in the equation are terms of force. If the equation is to make any sense, \(f(t)\) must also be a force. However, \(f(t)\) doesn't directly relate to the position at all: the dependent variable \(x\) doesn't show up. Therefore, \(f(t)\) must be a force which is not dependent on the current position, velocity or acceleration of the system. The only option is an external form on the system. In this way, homogenous equations represent a system free of external forces and non-homogeneous equations represent a system under the influence of an external force.
