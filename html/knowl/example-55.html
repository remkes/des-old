Example 5.1.4.

The Laplace transform of \(t^n\) for \(n \in \ZZ\) involved factorials, so it is not surprising that \(t^\alpha\) for non-integer \(\alpha\) involves the extension of the factorial: the \(\Gamma\) function.

\begin{align*} \calL \{ t^\alpha \} \amp = \int_0^\infty e^{-st} t^{\alpha} dt\\ \amp = \int_0^\infty t^{(\alpha + a) - 1} e^{-st}\\ u \amp = st\\ \amp = \int_0^\infty \left( \frac{u}{s} \right)^{(\alpha+1)-1} e^{-u} \frac{du}{s}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{s^{\alpha +1}} \int_0^\infty u^{(\alpha+ 1)-1} e^{-u} du = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha +1)}{s^{\alpha +1}} \end{align*}
