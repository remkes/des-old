The previous example was a logistic equation. The general form of the logistic equation is

\begin{equation*} \frac{dp}{dt} = cp(p-K) \end{equation*}

for some constants \(c\) and \(K\) with \(K \gt 0\text{.}\) The logistic equation leads to logistic growth. logistic growth, \(p = K\) is always a steady state and the starting values of the population always want to revert to this steady state. \(K\) is called the carrying capacity of the population. From below, the population wants to grow up to the carrying capacity. From above, the population wants to decay down to the carrying capacity. After exponential growth, logistic growth is the most commonly used model for populations.
