If we integrate \(f(t) \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{\pi}} e^{-at^2}\text{,}\) we get a weighted average of \(f(t)\) values near \(a\text{.}\) In the limit, though, only the value at \(f(a)\) matters, since we multiply by zero everywhere else. This gives the most convenient proprety of \(\delta\)-function.

\begin{equation*} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \delta_a (t) f(t) dt = f(a) \end{equation*}
